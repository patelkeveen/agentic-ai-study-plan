# 🚀 60-Day Production-Ready Agentic AI & Enterprise DevOps Study Plan

Welcome to your personalized, high-intensity study plan designed to transition your background in Enterprise IT and Security into a world-class **Agentic AI & DevOps Engineering** role. 

This curriculum is structured for **60 days**, requiring a minimum commitment of **5 hours per day (300 hours total)**. It is structured into three progressive stages to accelerate your career transition:
1. **Days 1–10: Job-Ready Foundation** (Essential coding, databases, automation APIs, and basic AI tooling).
2. **Days 11–30: Leveling Up & Essential Projects** (Infrastructure as Code, Cloud security governance, and stateful AI graph agents).
3. **Days 31–60: Deepening Expertise & Complex Projects** (Production Kubernetes, AI Guardrails, telemetry, automated Evals, and an enterprise capstone project).

---

## 🎯 Core Curriculum Objectives
* **AI Orchestration & Architecture**: Master LangGraph and LangChain to build stateful, multi-actor, production-ready AI agents.
* **Modern SaaS Stack Integration**: Build systems using Supabase (Postgres, RLS, Edge Functions), n8n (automation engine), Cal.com, Documenso, and NocoDB.
* **Advanced Agentic Memory & Guardrails**: Implement complex episodic memory, decay scoring, vector hybrid search, and robust guardrails (NeMo, Bedrock, Llama Firewall).
* **Enterprise DevOps & Security**: Deploy and manage Kubernetes (EKS), Docker, Coolify, Terraform, and secure them with Entra ID, Zero Trust, Sentinel SIEM, and SOC 2 guidelines.

## ⏱️ Daily Study Split (5 Hours)
To maximize retention and build production-ready skills, divide your 5 hours daily as follows:
* **Hour 1 & 2: Theoretical Foundation & Deep Dives (2 Hours)**
  * Read official documentation, research papers, and architectural guides.
  * Analyze open-source codebases (e.g., Cal.com, Documenso, LangGraph examples).
* **Hour 3 & 4: Hands-On Lab & Implementation (2 Hours)**
  * Build, code, configure, and break things. 
  * Commit daily exercises to this repository.
* **Hour 5: Tracing, Debugging & Documentation (1 Hour)**
  * Use tools like Langfuse, Logfire, or Datadog to observe what you built.
  * Refactor code, fix lint errors, and write up notes/lessons learned.
